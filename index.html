Abstract

The purpose of our project is to continue the development of the previous year's Electric Go-Kart. Our project is closely connected with the ECE Outreach Team, with the main purpose of the go-kart being educating students about engineering concepts.
Our core goals this year are focused on increasing the dimensions of electrical and computer engineering covered in the kart. By redesigning our controls platform to enable more sensors and compute power, we plan to offer a wealth of ECE application demos for student enjoyment.

Background

Last year's team presented us with a fully-functional electric go-kart. The kart features a battery system with two electric motors, a custom chassis, and an embedded controls system for data readout. Feel free to check out current pictures of the kart and it's progress on the pictures page.

Design Goals

  • Redesign the power system to better protect the components from damage
  • Design and manufacture a new chassis that improves upon the current
  • Integrate the identification system to the rest of the kart to achieve autonomous functions
  • Improve the ESC with better programing and safety features to increase the maximum speed of the GoKart

Project Constraints

  • While new chassis is being developed, all systems must be tested on old chassis
  • Current chassis lacks various safety precautious that require the team to account for before or during improvements
  • The time elapsed since the previous team's departure has left many firmwares and programs out of date and will require updating
